Week 2 Discussion Question

Industry Research and Project Intent

    What are some of the ways that you could change your HTML on your Demo app to be more accessible? (Remember, you can use the Section tag with jQuery, for instance)

    Since my CRUD app is about logging in beer, I would make sure that the app is accessible to those that are drunk. One way to accomplish this would be to make sure buttons are spaced far apart so a user would no accidentally click on something when motor skills have been impaired.

    Visit a video service on the web (like YouTube) and search for "Screen Reader Demo" and "Mobile Screen Reader" - what are some of the common frustrations (or common support mechanisms) that you see among the examples that are given - how could you fix them (or use them)?

    First let me point out, the firs thing these screen reader companies need is better demos. That being said, it is hard for me to really imagine being blind and not being able to see what is on the screen. As a developer I could make sure that my applications interface with those screen readers. Not only will this increase my audience of users to those perhaps my competition has not considered, but I will have made someone's life that much easier.

    Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

    • Benheck's Access Controller
      The "Access" Controller is an Xbox360 Controller designed for those who only have one hand. The controller is of modular design so you can move the elements (d=pad, joystick, buttons, etc..) to any location.

    • iPad Mounting System
      The iPad Mounting System is an easy to use device that clamps on to a wheel chair or motorized chair. The mount hold the iPad in a custom case that eases the use of the device for those with the lack of motor skills.

    Mobile Apps: Please list two apps (either Android or iOS, or both) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

    • iComm
      Initially designed for children under three. This application uses pictures and words so a person who has trouble communicating through words can express themselves. Helpful for those with cerebral palsy or autism.

    • iSpectrum Color Blind Assistant
      iSpectrum is a photo app for those who are color blind. You take a picture of the item you would like to identify, click on that part of the picture and the application gives you a spectrum and name of the color selected.

Market Research

    What are the 'top selling' app categories in the U.S.? Japan? the UK?

    All these markets are very similar with games being the number one sellers. There are some abnormalities here and there, but the clear front runner is games.

    Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

    That the global app market is beginning to look very similar. With games being the top apps, it seems most people across the world are using there phones for entertainment rather than business. This is a giant shift over the past 10 years where the majority of people who owned cellular phones used them for business.

    What percentage of Android users have the latest/greatest OS in the U.S? in Japan? in Great Britain? How might these differences impact development?

    10.9% are using the latest and greatest in the US. I was unable to discover specifics for Japan or Great Britain, so the impact could not be discussed.

    What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

    USA: $3.33 USD per 1mb
    JAPAN: $0.27 USD per 1mb
    UK: $3.00 USD per 1mb
    The USA has the most costly data plans of any other country. India appears to be the lowest at the moment. This greatly impacts development, as if you are on a pay-per- use plan you do not want to download large applications or applications that access large files such as YouTube videos.

    To maximize revenue. I would make a game that has social networking elements tied to it. This would hit the second highest category in the US. The US contains the majority of app purchasers. A cost comparison would have to be done, to see if translating the app to Japanese would be relevant. But English being a common language in the UK and USA you could deploy the app in both countries.
